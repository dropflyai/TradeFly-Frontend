Entry: $135.20 ‚Üí Target: $155.48 (Stop: $128.44)

Confidence: 85% ‚Ä¢ Risk/Reward: 3.00x

Breaking It Down:

  1. NVDA $50 CALL
    This is the option contract. "CALL" means you profit if NVDA stock goes UP. "$50" is the strike price (ignore this for now - focus on the entry/exit prices).
  2. Exp: 2025-12-12
    Expiration date. You need to close the trade BEFORE this date (usually same day or next day for these scalps).
  3. Entry: $135.20
    This is what you PAY per contract. One contract = $135.20 √ó 100 = $13,520
  4. Target: $155.48
    SELL HERE for profit. One contract = $155.48 √ó 100 = $15,548
    Profit = $15,548 - $13,520 = $2,028 (15% gain)
  5. Stop: $128.44
    EXIT HERE if trade goes against you to limit losses.
    Loss = $13,520 - $12,844 = -$676 (5% loss)
  6. Confidence: 85%
    How confident the system is. Higher = better. Look for 70%+ signals.
  7. Risk/Reward: 3.00x
    For every $1 you risk, you can make $3. Higher is better.

‚ùì Common Questions

Q: What does "BUY_CALL" mean?

Buy a CALL option = you profit when the stock goes UP.

Q: What does "BUY_PUT" mean?

Buy a PUT option = you profit when the stock goes DOWN.

Q: Why do prices multiply by 100?

One option contract controls 100 shares. So $1.35 per share = $135 per contract.

Q: Should I trade every signal?

NO. Only trade signals with 70%+ confidence. Max 2-3 positions at once.

Q: When do I enter the trade?

As soon as you see the signal during market hours (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET).

Q: How long do I hold?

These are SCALPING signals: 1-5 minutes to a few hours. Exit same day.

Q: What if I can't afford a full contract?

Some brokers offer fractional contracts, or wait for smaller signals. Never risk more than you have.
